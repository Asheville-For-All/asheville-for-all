Buncombe County Bonds Referendum, Asheville City Council Agenda, and more

August 21, 2022

Get Asheville For All newsletters delivered to your inbox. Sign up here.

Buncombe County’s November Bonds Referendum

Buncombe County voters have two big bond propositions to vote on this November, and one of those bonds is all about getting more income restricted housing units (sometimes called “Affordable Housing”) built in the county.

Here’s a recent Mountain Xpress article about the bonds.

Income restricted housing is just one of the tools that we need in our toolbox if we’re going to make housing affordable for ALL Ashevilleans. Stay tuned for more on the bonds referendum as we enter the fall.

Asheville City Council Agenda on Tuesday

There are two items of interest on the council’s agenda on Tuesday, and you may want to call or email the council to express your support.

First, a housing development is being planned for 319 Biltmore Ave. All housing is needed in a scarcity crisis, but we think this apartment project is notable for its location—it is close to jobs and transit—and for its mixed use design.

Second, the Council will be voting to approve the South Slope Vision Plan.

We like the emphasis on “urban character” in this plan, which includes mixed uses, upzoning, and multi-modal transportation.

You can find instructions on how to send the City Council an email or leave them a recorded message here.

Upcoming Online Event: “How to Tackle Climate Change and Housing Affordability”

Our parent organization, YIMBY Action, regularly hosts advocacy training webinars for free, and the next one coming up is on “How to Tackle Climate Change and Housing Affordability.”

The online event will be at 2pm on Sunday, September 11th, and anyone can join by signing up here.

You don’t have to have attended any prior trainings/webinars!

Here’s the full description:

What if we could tackle climate change and make housing more affordable at the same time?

When we advocate for denser housing options, walkable neighborhoods, and robust public transit systems, we reduce carbon emissions while making our communities healthier and more resilient. This workshop will teach you how housing advocacy and climate action go hand in hand.

Come learn how we can tackle climate change and housing affordability at the same time!

ICYMI: Buncombe County Survey

In case you haven’t seen this already:

Buncombe County has a new survey about the “2043 comprehensive plan“ available for residents to complete. “Land Use & Housing” is the first section on the survey. (You don’t need to complete all of the survey’s sections.)

Click here to take the survey.

The deadline is September 6.
