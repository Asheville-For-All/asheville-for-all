Statement on Asheville’s Water Crisis

Asheville For All issued an official statement on how NOT to respond to the water crisis that affected Asheville and Buncombe County residents in late December and early January.

You can read it here.

Vacation Rental Roundtable

Our lead organizers convened a virtual “roundtable” discussion, all about the subject of vacation rentals such as “AirBnBs” in Asheville.

Are vacation rentals bad for the city? What should we do, if anything, to try to regulate them? And what does any of this have to do with the city’s housing crisis?

We agreed on some questions, and disagreed on others! You can read the conversation here.

Upcoming Online Event: “How to Make Housing Affordable for Everyone”

Asheville For All’s parent organization, YIMBY Action, regularly hosts advocacy training webinars for free, and the next one coming up is on “How to Make Housing Affordable for Everyone.”

Here’s the description:

Ever wondered why it’s so expensive to buy a home? Or why rent is so damn high?

You’re not alone. Housing has been expensive for a long time, and prices have only continued to rise in recent years. The good news is that we can make homes affordable for everyone, and you can join us!

Come learn how homes got so expensive and how you can join the fight for abundant, affordable housing.

The online event will be at 2pm on Thursday, January 26th, and anyone can tune in by signing up here.

Join Us at the Buncombe County TDA Meeting

Asheville For All members will be delivering public comments at the next meeting of the Buncombe County Tourism Development Authority (BCTDA), in support of a proposal to reorient funds collected from the county’s occupancy tax towards subsidizing the construction of more multifamily homes for working people.

The meeting will take place on Wednesday, January 25th at 9am, and information about providing comment can be found here. Let us know if you would like to attend with us!

Buncombe Decides is leading the effort to see the BCTDA’s funds spent on more equitable pursuits such as subsidized housing construction. You can find more information at their website.
