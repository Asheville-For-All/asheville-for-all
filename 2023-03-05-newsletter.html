ICYMI: Support the Effort to Build Housing for Buncombe County’s Service Workers

In case you missed it, we’ve joined Buncombe Decides, Asheville Food & Beverage United, Asheville DSA, and other Buncombe County community members to put forth a petition directed towards the Buncombe County Tourism Development Authority (BCTDA).

The BCTDA has an opportunity, given a recent change in how the state government mandates how it spends the money that it receives from the county’s occupancy tax, to help out with the housing crisis.

Read more and sign the petition here.

(You can also find more information in a recent post on our blog and at Buncombe Decides.)

This petition presents practical and common-sense asks. Please take a few seconds to sign, and please share it with your friends, family members, and neighbors too.

Online Event: “Public Comment Workshop” with YIMBY Action

Asheville For All’s parent organization, YIMBY Action, regularly hosts advocacy training webinars for free, and the next one coming up is a “Public Comment Workshop.”

Here’s the description:

Want to support new housing in your community and convince your neighbors to join you?

Public comments are a great opportunity for people to advocate for more housing. Historically, single-family homeowners have used public forums to block the creation of new housing. But when you use your voice to say you support more housing, city officials are more likely to allow more housing to be built. Plus you’ll help encourage others to do the same!

Come learn how to give a persuasive public comment at community meetings and help get more housing built.

The online event will be at 2pm on Thursday, March 9th. Click here to RSVP.
