Missing Middle Support Opportunity, Free Event This Wednesday + more

June 25, 2023

Get Asheville For All newsletters delivered to your inbox. Sign up here.

Save the Date: “Missing Middle Housing Workshop” Public Input Session

We’ve received advance word that the City of Asheville and Opticos will be holding an “open house” on Friday, August 4th from 4pm to 7pm at the Harrah’s Center Banquet Hall.

(Not sure what “missing middle housing” means? Check out our info page!)

If you live in Asheville and care about the housing shortage, this may be our next opportunity for showing public support for the missing middle initiative. More details as they come!

Member Commentary: “What About the Dream of Owning a Home?: How More ‘Missing Middle’ Homes Can Help Homebuyers Too”

One of our lead organizers has thoughts on how “missing middle” reforms will benefit not just renters but home buyers too.

Check it out on our blog.

Online Event: “Save the Planet: Build More Homes!” with YIMBY Action

Asheville For All’s parent organization, YIMBY Action, regularly hosts advocacy training webinars for free, and the next one coming up is “Save the Planet: Build More Homes!”

Here’s the description:

What if we could tackle climate change and make housing more affordable at the same time?

When we advocate for denser housing options, walkable neighborhoods, and robust public transit systems, we reduce carbon emissions while making our communities healthier and more resilient. This workshop will teach you how housing advocacy and climate action go hand in hand.

Come learn how we can tackle climate change and housing affordability at the same time!

The online event will be at 2pm on Tuesday, July 11th. Click here to RSVP.

Local Event of Interest: Asheville’s Urban Design Speaker Series - This Wednesday

The Collider in Downtown Asheville is hosting speaker Eric Kronberg this Wednesday at 6:00pm. (Doors will open at 5:30pm.)

This is the latest event in an ongoing series, called “Building Our City,” and the 2023 lineup is focused on housing. Hailing from Atlanta, Kronberg is expected to speak about the obstacles that zoning codes present to getting more livable, walkable communities built.

You can learn more and RSVP to the free event here.

(Asheville For All is not in any way affiliated with this event. Though we are excited to attend!)
