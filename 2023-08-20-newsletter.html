What Should Asheville’s “Missing Middle” Housing Look Like Exactly?

As a recent article about the success of New Zealand’s upzoning efforts noted, “the devil is in the details” when it comes to land use reform.

So, according to one of our lead organizers, in addition to the question of “should Asheville undertake a ‘missing middle’ rezoning?” we’ll need to ask other questions about what that might look like. You can read more in a commentary post on our blog.

Come hang out with Asheville For All!

We’ll be gathering on Saturday, August 26th, from 2:00pm-4:00pm, at the Hi-Wire Brewing “Beer Garden” on Lyman St. in the River Arts District, for an informal meetup.

Anyone interested in housing abundance is welcome to join. We hope to see you there. Feel free to bring friends and family members too.

Click here to RSVP and let us know that you’re coming.

More details to come.

We also plan to have a little celebration afterwards! Click here to learn more and to RSVP.

Online Event: “How to Achieve Racial Justice in Housing” with YIMBY Action - Thursday, September 14th

Asheville For All’s parent organization, YIMBY Action, regularly hosts advocacy training webinars for free, and the next one coming up is on “How to Achieve Racial Justice in Housing.”

Here’s the description:

Did you know that our neighborhoods are more segregated today than they were in 1990?

For decades, racist housing laws throughout the country have historically excluded people of color from wealthy, predominantly white neighborhoods. This has kept folks away from homeownership, high-paying jobs, high-quality schools, and other key opportunities to help families thrive. This webinar will go through the history of these racist policies, how they perpetuate segregation today, and how we can achieve racial justice in housing.

Come learn why neighborhoods are still segregated and how we can make housing equitable for everyone.

This online event is on Thursday, September 14th, at 7pm. Click here to RSVP.
